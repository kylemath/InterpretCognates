Validation & Literature Tests

Tests against external benchmarks from cognitive science and historical linguistics. Each experiment validates a specific hypothesis about cross-lingual semantic structure.

⚠ Compute Warning: These experiments are computationally expensive. Each test embeds thousands of words and may take 2–5 minutes. Results are not cached across page reloads.
1

Isotropy Correction Test

Does the Swadesh convergence ranking hold under isotropy (ABTT) correction? A stable ranking indicates the signal is geometric, not an artifact of anisotropy.

~2–5 min
2

Swadesh vs Non-Swadesh Comparison

Does core vocabulary (Swadesh) converge significantly more than culturally-specific vocabulary? A significant difference supports the cultural stability hypothesis.

~2–5 min
3

Colexification Test (CLICS²)

Do concept pairs that are frequently colexified across languages (e.g., hand–arm) show higher embedding similarity than never-colexified pairs? Tests universal conceptual associations.

~2–5 min
4

Conceptual Store Metric

Does mean-centering (subtracting per-language means) reorganize the embedding space from language clusters to concept clusters? The conceptual store hypothesis (Correia et al., 2014) predicts a ≥2× improvement in between/within concept distance ratio.

~2–5 min
5

Phylogenetic Distance & Mantel Test

Do embedding distances between languages correlate with phylogenetic (phonetic) distance? Includes Mantel test against approximate ASJP LDND distances, conceptual maps per language family, MDS projection, dendrogram, and 3D PCA of language centroids.

~2–5 min
6

Semantic Offset Invariance

Are semantic relationships translationally invariant? For concept pairs like man→woman, we compute the offset vector in each language independently and measure whether these offsets are parallel across languages. High consistency means the relationship is a universal property of the conceptual space, not language-specific.

~3 min
